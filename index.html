The Platform

The ESS has ballooned in recent years. We've moved from some 40 volunteers to nearly 150 in the past two years. New initatives have been proposed, with some seeing more success than others. Without a doubt, the ESS is one of the strongest Faculty Associations on Campus, and one of strongest Engineering Societies in Canada. This couldn't have been done without the strength of previous ESS executives, presidents, and the legacies they have left behind. Their work in moving the ESS forward, relinquishing the closed-mindedness and petty immaturies of the past, cannot be overstated.

My platform is one of unity, of retrospection. This year alone has seen many new iniatives: GEER Pass, Beer Gardens, GEER Store renovations, Job Shadow Program, First Year Retreat. & a revamped Engg Ball. This has, of course, spread the ESS thin. My Presidency would see a return to form for the ESS, we're finally in a good spot. Our finances are in order, our relationship with the Faculty is more than just amiable. We need to take a step back and focus on strengthen our existing iniatives, services, and events. The more iniatives, the more people, and the more people means more bueacracy for things that we haven't completely nailed down.

Every expansion is followed with a period of celebration & vindication of ones efforts. I would see our efforts be put towards engaging students with what we have, rather than introducing fluff that they're disinterested in. Better transition, better intra-club relations, more transparency. It's time for us to celebrate.
